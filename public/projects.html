ANDROID STUDIO PROJECT

Mealer is an extraordinary food ordering app that provides a one-stop-shop solution for clients to order their favorite meals from exceptional chefs near them. This android app was built as part of the course SEG2105 at University of Ottawa by Group 4.

CLIENT PAGE

As a client, you can search for meals you want to order.

CHEF PAGE

As a chef, you can offer meals and accept or reject client orders.

ADMIN PAGE

As an admin, you deal with client complaints made about chefs.

KNEADING HISTORY WEBSITE

Kneading Bread is a fake website on the history of bread, constructed using HTML and CSS for a digital humanities course. The purpose of the project was to see how realistic looking an amateur can make a website look within 5 hours. It was a test to see how simple it is for a regular person to spread misinformation through an official looking site.

The website includes all basic historical site elements: a navigation bar, text+images, links, sections and a footer.

To speed up site creation, prompts were fed into a text generator for the article text. This led to some interesting results.

The website can be accessed at DHN_Bread_Site.

WORDLE

Don't let them kill your sparkle✧˖°

This Wordle game was created as part of the course CSI 3140 at University of Ottawa.
Version 1 of Wordle was created using HTML, CSS and JavaScript. Version 2 was elevated by incorporating PHP through the usage of AJAX calls. Thus allowing for partial screen updates to occur.
Similar to the New York Times Wordle game, the player is given 6 chances to guess the correct five-letter word.

Users type their answers using their keyboard and hit 'Enter' when they have a guess.
Each guess is graded with a colour to indicate the correctness of the guess.

When the player guesses the correct word, an alert appears with their congratulations message.

Users can keep track of their top 10 scores by viewing the scoreboard.

After either guessing the Wordle or running out of guesses, the player is given the option to play again by clicking the 'PLAY AGAIN?' button.
